Our Research

Our team is proposing to develop an intelligent computer vision-based interactive netball training platform that addresses the comprehensive needs of netball development through advanced Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Image Processing technologies. This innovative platform focuses on identifying and developing key aspects of netball performance including talents, skills, fitness levels, netball passes, and hand signal recognition in sports development, while equipping users with essential theoretical knowledge to achieve competitive success and optimal performance in competitions. The platform targets a diverse range of stakeholders including coaches, netball players, netball organizations, and referees, providing tailored solutions for each user group's specific requirements. This study proposes a predictive approach utilizing cutting-edge image and video processing technology that moves beyond traditional training methods, offering real-time analysis and feedback to enhance player development and game performance. By integrating computer vision capabilities with sports science principles, the platform aims to revolutionize netball training methodologies, providing data-driven insights that support both individual skill development and team strategy optimization across all levels of the sport.

Our Research

Introduction

Netball is a dynamic team sport requiring physical, technical, and strategic skills for optimal performance. Originating in the late 19th century as a basketball variant, it's globally recognized, particularly in Commonwealth nations like Australia, New Zealand, and the UK. The game features two seven-player teams on a rectangular court divided into thirds with goal circles at each end. Each player's movement is restricted to specific court areas across seven positions: Center (C), Wing Attack (WA), Wing Defense (WD), Goal Attack (GA), Goal Shooter (GS), Goal Defense (GD), and Goal Keeper (GK). Key rules include passing or shooting within three seconds of possession, with no running or dribbling allowed. Accurate ball passing significantly increases winning chances, while umpires make final match decisions. Athletes must develop five basic skills: ball handling, footwork, attacking, defending, and shooting. Coaches assess these during practice sessions using essential coaching points, determining suitable player positions and team strategy alignment. Effective training programs including netball-specific skills serve as injury prevention strategies. This research covers three motor skills: power, coordination, and balance, assessed through exercises like power squats, agility zig-zag runs, and coordination ladder drills. Key passing techniques analyzed include chest pass, overhead pass, shoulder pass, side pass, and underarm pass. Essential umpire hand signals for Start, Timeout, and Direction of Pass support clear communication and fair play. Our research addresses traditional evaluation method limitations by developing a data-driven system enhancing objectivity, precision, and efficiency. Unlike subjective coaching approaches with manual data collection, our system utilizes machine learning algorithms, video analytics, and AI technologies including Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), pre-trained MobileNet models, MediaPipe pose detection, and OpenCV centroid motion detection to analyze key performance matrices.

Abstraction

Netball is a high-paced team sport requiring a blend of physical, technical, and strategic skills to optimize performance. Traditional player evaluation methods rely on subjective coaching expertise and manual data collection, which can be time consuming and prone to bias. This research introduces a data-driven approach to skill assessment and position prediction, leveraging machine learning and computer vision to enhance the accuracy and efficiency of player evaluation. Our system utilizes deep learning models, including Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) and Long Short Term Memory (LSTM), alongside pre-trained Mobile Net models, Media pipe pose detection, and OpenCV centroid motion tracking. These technologies enable automated assessment of essential netball skills such as passing techniques, motor skills (power, coordination, and balance), and umpire hand signals for real-time gameplay evaluation. The study also incorporates structured position assignments, ensuring that players are placed in roles that align with their abilities. By integrating AI-powered analytics, this research aims to revolutionize netball training and performance analysis, providing coaches with objective, data-driven insights to refine player development and optimize team trategy.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Real-time Feedback.

Current System: Provides feedback within 2-5 seconds using MediaPipe and OpenCV with 93% movement tracking accuracy. Limitations: Performance drops to 78% in poor lighting; requires stable camera setup and minimum 720p resolution.

Performance Metrics.

Metrics Tracked: Motor skills (power, coordination, balance), fundamental skills (ball handling, footwork, attacking, defending, shooting), and position-specific requirements. Long-term Impact: Shows 25% improvement in 4 weeks and successful skill-to-position mapping, but lacks extended longitudinal tracking.

Skill-Level Customization.

Current Approach: Uses fixed thresholds and position-specific requirements (GS needs 90-100% shooting accuracy). Limitations: Static criteria without adaptive learning; does not account for individual development patterns or age\experience differences.

Training Data Limitations.

Dataset Scope: School-based players, indoor/outdoor environments, multiple camera angles covering 3 umpire signals and 3 pass types. Constraints: Limited professional player data, controlled settings may not reflect real match conditions, potential overfitting despite high accuracy rates (89-100%).

Proposed Problem

Netball training today is hindered by traditional, subjective coaching methods that lack the precision and personalization needed for modern athletic performance enhancement. As the sport evolves, there is a clear technological gap in assessing, improving, and tracking player performance through data-driven approaches. The primary challenge lies in collecting and extracting relevant performance features. This research proposes a robust data collection framework integrating high-resolution cameras, wearable motion sensors, and advanced computer vision to capture detailed metrics such as hand signal accuracy, passing precision, body positioning, and biomechanical efficiency. These data points provide a comprehensive view of player capabilities and improvement areas. Developing machine learning models is essential for converting raw data into actionable insights. The project aims to create multi-modal models using computer vision, deep learning, and statistical analysis tailored to netball's specific needs classifying hand signals, analyzing passing techniques and player positioning, and tracking fitness levels. Techniques like transfer learning and domain-specific datasets will enable intelligent systems to deliver in-depth performance analysis. The ultimate goal is to build a digital training ecosystem that replaces traditional methods with personalized skill assessments, Umpire Hand Signals, and dynamic training recommendations. The platform will identify player strengths and weaknesses, generate custom workout plans, and offer real-time feedback to support ongoing improvement. Innovations include precise gesture tracking via computer vision, predictive modeling through machine learning, and adaptive training systems. These will democratize high-performance training, offering data-driven development tools across all skill levels and potentially revolutionizing netball training. Challenges include building sport-specific datasets, developing accurate and generalizable models, ensuring user-friendliness, and addressing ethical concerns around data privacy. This research not only provides a technical solution but also promotes a shift toward more objective, systematic, and personalized player development. By strategically applying advanced technologies, this project aims to unlock new levels of athletic potential and reshape netball training through an intelligent, adaptive ecosystem that supports continuous player growth.

Proposed Solution

The research solution presents an AI-driven netball performance platform that revolutionizes training through comprehensive video analysis and intelligent feedback systems using advanced computer vision and deep learning technologies. Motor Skills Assessment employs CNN-LSTM models with 98% accuracy to evaluate physical capabilities through standardized exercises (squats, 360-degree jumps, ladder drills), automatically tracking joint angles and movement quality while providing real-time corrective feedback. Position Prediction System combines ResNet50 spatial extraction with LSTM temporal analysis to recognize five fundamental skills (ball handling, footwork, attacking, defending, shooting). Random Forest classification predicts optimal court positions, enabling data-driven team formation strategies. Movement Tracking implements CNN models to analyze netball passes (chest, bounce, shoulder), comparing execution against reference standards and providing accuracy scores with specific feedback on posture and technique. Umpire Signal Recognition uses YOLOv8 object detection with custom CNN architecture achieving 89% accuracy for Start/Restart, Direction of Pass, and Timeout signals, supporting standardized officiating development. The integrated web-based platform delivers seamless video upload, real-time processing, and detailed analytics through a user-friendly interface. Players receive personalized training recommendations and longitudinal progress tracking, while coaches gain objective assessment tools that eliminate subjective bias. This comprehensive approach transforms traditional observation-based training into precise, data-driven performance enhancement that accelerates skill acquisition and maximizes athletic potential.
